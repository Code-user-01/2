Bonjour!
Un poco sobre mí...

Nací y crecí en Tours, Francia, donde desarrollé mi amor por los idiomas. Después de terminar la escuela secundaria, decidí seguir mi pasión y estudié lenguas extranjeras. Esto me llevó a vivir experiencias enriquecedoras en Irlanda e Italia, donde no solo perfeccioné mis habilidades lingüísticas, sino también mi capacidad para adaptarme a nuevas culturas. En 2011, obtuve mi título de posgrado en francés e italiano en la Universidad Oxford Brookes, y fue allí donde comencé a explorar el fascinante mundo de la enseñanza, trabajando con estudiantes de todas las edades.

Mi carrera me llevó a la Alianza Francesa en Cebú, Filipinas, donde impartí clases de francés general para adultos y especializadas en enfermería y turismo para estudiantes universitarios. Durante este tiempo, descubrí la importancia de hacer el aprendizaje accesible y emocionante, integrando la cultura francesa en el aula a través de materiales auténticos, tecnología y recursos visuales que enriquecen la experiencia educativa de cada estudiante.

Mi siguiente aventura fue en la Escuela Extranjera de Seúl, Corea del Sur, donde tuve el privilegio de crecer como educadora en una comunidad internacional solidaria. A lo largo de mi carrera, he creído firmemente que los estudiantes aprenden mejor cuando se comunican activamente en el idioma que están aprendiendo. Por eso, siempre busco incorporar métodos interactivos y lúdicos, como juegos, canciones y teatro, para que el aprendizaje sea tanto efectivo como divertido.

Después, me mudé a Colombia, donde trabajé en el Colegio Anglo Colombiano en el Programa de la Escuela Primaria (PAI) y el Bachillerato Internacional (IB). Enseñar a estudiantes de diversos contextos culturales me permitió reevaluar y mejorar mi enseñanza continuamente. La llegada de la pandemia me obligó a adaptarme una vez más, encontrando maneras creativas de seguir transmitiendo mi amor por los idiomas en un entorno virtual.

A lo largo de mi trayectoria, he descubierto que trabajar en el entorno de una escuela internacional es una experiencia increíblemente gratificante. Colaborar con personas de diferentes orígenes, pero unidas por el deseo de aprender, me ha inspirado profundamente. Estoy comprometida con seguir desarrollando mis habilidades y haciendo una diferencia positiva en la vida de mis estudiantes.

Teaching Philosophy

I have a passion for Foreign Languages and strongly believe languages contribute to a sense of mutual understanding and personal fulfilment. My goal is to convey my enthusiasm for languages by creating a relaxed and positive learning atmosphere where pupils can get highly involved in a wide range of activities and opportunities, whilst being exposed to French.

I believe pupils benefit from their language skills first and foremost as a way to encourage positive attitudes, respect towards diversity, and help them become world citizens. Bearing this in mind, I try to develop pupils’ cultural awareness by teaching French not only with a focus on France, but on the entire Francophone community.

"Clear learning objectives and success criteria empower students to take responsibility for their own learning."

Furthermore, I find that clear learning objectives and success criteria, which can be shared with students, allow them to take more responsibility for their own learning and achievements. With that objective in mind, I always ensure my pupils understand the different curriculum levels so they can set their own learning targets and become independent learners.

The ability to understand and communicate in another language represents a challenge in itself. Therefore, I always encourage my students through rewards and positive praise, and convey the idea that making mistakes is an important part of the route to language success. Every pupil’s contribution is valued in my class, and students are encouraged to take risks with their learning.

"Caring about each student’s well-being and achievement is central to my teaching."

Caring about each student’s well-being and achievement, I believe effective teaching comes from understanding individual needs, learning styles, and personalities. My time in school has shown me the importance of planning for progression and developing individualised learning plans that allow pupils to make progress at their own pace, building confidence.

To me, a successful lesson is one in which students are fully engaged and enjoying the learning process. This is why I strive to employ a variety of teaching methods, such as interactive games, cooperative learning, storytelling, and drama, to make learning as fun as possible. I aim to inspire students and instill in them a lasting love for languages.

In the classroom

Year 1-2 (British School)

Over the last few years I have also increasingly been using TPRS techniques, especially at the lower levels. I found that students were able to produce and play with the language more than before. It is therefore an area I have been training in and wish to master some day.

In Year 1 and 2, I help my students learn the language through stories, rhymes, playground games and songs. At this level, they learn basic vocabulary, short phrases and sentences to communicate with others in the target language.

Everyday, my lessons are focused on developing their Listening and Speaking skills. The students having a short attention span, each 30-minute lesson has a minimum of 6 different activities and routines well in place, in order to maintain the students' engagement and interest.

The Y1-2 class learning how to introduce oneself in French.

Year 3-4 (British School)

Being in an International School, many students leave after a couple of years, so I rarely have students coming from the Y1/2 class joining the Y3/4 French class. I have therefore been teaching this class basics, using similar techniques as with the Y1/2 class (songs, games, stories), but with an introduction to Reading and Writing.

Again, TPRS techniques have been very successful at this level, and students have enjoyed learning through stories which they were able to then perform.

Year 5/6

I teach Y5 and 6 students French through a diversity of oral and practical activities (songs, games, skits) with the addition of reading and writing as they progress.

At that level, the students were placed in classes according to their proficiency level. The lower level focuses on consolidating basics to ensure a strong foundation for Middle School, while the higher level also starts working on manipulating the written language by studying some grammatical structures.

Novice/Phase 1

French Novice/Phase 1 is an introduction to the French language for students who have never studied the language. Students are highly encouraged at this level to speak as much French as possible in class.

In the past, I have organized language exchanges with another school in France for that level. My students have therefore been able to apply their learning in real life situations and had a greater sense of purpose. During each unit, students would send either a video or a letter to their penpal about different aspects of their lives (home, family, hobbies, town, and food). We were able to send each other's letters, videos, real postcards, and even food!

In addition, those exchanges have been another way to formatively assess my students and be more aware of their strengths and weaknesses.

Intermediate/Phase 2-3

Those higher levels have been a continuation of the Novice/Phase 1 course, focusing on expanding reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills. At this stage, the students have had at least between 1 to 2 years of French, and I highly encourage them to speak only in the target language in class. Although the knowledge expectations are increasingly more complex, I strive to keep my lessons fun, engaging, and interesting to the students by incorporating games, group projects, language exchanges, musical projects, and as much cultural content as possible for them to discover.

One of the padlet sent to our penpals: in this particular one, students had to send a video about the PyeongChang 2018 Winter Olympics, which many had been attending. By doing so, they could recycle topics studied such as family members, sports & hobbies, likes & dislikes, and past tense.

Manie Musicale activities & project

Every year, the amazing teachers Stephanie Carbonneau and Michelle Fournier, have been organizing the Manie Musicale competition, in which students of French all around the world vote for the best out of 16 current songs (and predict the results).

This competition has been by far a favorite of all of my classes, which is why each year, I decided to extend it a bit by asking my students to recreate one of those songs in groups of 4/5 as part of their final assessment. They love being able to recreate a musical video and each year I am amazed by their creativity and passion for this particular task. Throughout the project they get to discover further famous French-speaking artists, study the meaning of lyrics and learn by heart one of those songs. However, the most rewarding aspect is being able to hear students all over campus singing French songs in the corridors and classrooms months and even years after the project.
